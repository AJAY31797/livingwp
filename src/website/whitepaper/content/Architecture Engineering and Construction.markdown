**Title**

AI Adoption in the AEC Sector in New Zealand: A Living Whitepaper

---

**Introduction**

- **Scope window (this update): 18 December 2024–18 December 2025.** Publicly verifiable evidence within this 12‑month window indicates **select but material movement from experimentation to early operational use** of AI in New Zealand’s AEC system, with the strongest public evidence concentrated in **asset-owner and public-sector AEC-adjacent functions** (utilities, councils, hazard agencies) rather than mainstream design-and-construct delivery workflows. ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/dmsdocument/263~full-results-2025-cross-agency-survey-for-artificial-intelligence-ai-use-cases/html?utm_source=openai))  
- **Observed adoption pattern (NZ):** AI is most clearly evidenced where there is:
  - **High consequence and time sensitivity** (flood inundation nowcasting for emergency response and infrastructure risk decisions). ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Large, instrumented networks** where AI can detect anomalies and prioritise interventions (wastewater network monitoring). ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Worksite safety monitoring** using computer vision / ML event detection (civil works pilot). ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Information access and service navigation** via LLM-enabled assistants and secure GenAI licences in local government. ([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Enabling environment:** National public-sector governance artefacts and investment signals became more explicit during the window, including:
  - **New Zealand’s AI Strategy (July 2025)**. ([mbie.govt.nz](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/economic-growth/digital-policy/new-zealands-ai-strategy-investing-with-confidence?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Public Service AI Framework (last updated 29 January 2025)** and **Responsible AI Guidance for the Public Service: GenAI** (updated guidance, date not consistently displayed on-page but publicly available as the current version within this period). ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/public-service-artificial-intelligence-framework?trk=public_post_comment-text&utm_source=openai))  
  - **NZIAT AI Research Platform concepts selected (published 18 December 2025; platform expected July 2026)** with concepts spanning areas including infrastructure and robotics. ([mbie.govt.nz](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/about/news/concepts-selected-for-ai-research-platform?utm_source=openai))  
- **Evidence limitations (NZ):** Public reporting is strongest on **what was piloted/deployed and why**, and weaker on:
  - **Operational performance and error rates** (false positives/negatives; model drift monitoring),
  - **assurance practice** (ongoing evaluation, incident handling),
  - **quantified impacts** (time savings, reduced overflows, measurable safety outcome changes),
  especially for **computer vision safety** and **LLM-enabled assistants**. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))

**Policy and Frameworks**

- **New Zealand’s AI Strategy: “Investing with confidence” (MBIE, July 2025).**
  - Establishes national framing oriented toward accelerating AI adoption and innovation, and includes responsible AI positioning (including reference alignment with OECD AI principles in associated MBIE communications). ([mbie.govt.nz](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/economic-growth/digital-policy/new-zealands-ai-strategy-investing-with-confidence?utm_source=openai))  
- **Public Service AI Framework (NZ Digital Government, last updated 29 January 2025).**
  - Provides a principles-led framework for responsible public-service AI use (relevant to AEC where councils/agencies use AI for asset operations, customer interfaces, planning/consenting support functions). ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/public-service-artificial-intelligence-framework?trk=public_post_comment-text&utm_source=openai))  
- **Responsible AI Guidance for the Public Service: GenAI (NZ Digital Government, current guidance available in 2025).**
  - Provides public-service GenAI guidance addressing governance, security, procurement, skills/capability, misinformation/hallucinations, and accountability, including citizen/customer experience considerations such as transparency. ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/responsible-ai-guidance-for-the-public-service-genai?utm_source=openai))  
- **Cross‑agency survey of AI use cases (GCDO, survey June 2025; report last updated 11 August 2025).**
  - Reports **70 agencies** and **272 AI use cases**, with **55 operational**, up from **15 operational in 2024**, indicating increased operationalisation and solution reuse across government—relevant context for AEC-facing agencies and infrastructure owners. ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/dmsdocument/263~full-results-2025-cross-agency-survey-for-artificial-intelligence-ai-use-cases/html?utm_source=openai))  
- **NZIAT (MBIE): Concepts selected for AI Research Platform (published 18 December 2025; expected establishment July 2026; up to $70m over 7 years).**
  - Signals a major national capability investment; selected concepts include agentic AI, autonomous intelligence “at the edge”, and physical AI for real-world systems, with explicit reference to sectors including infrastructure and robotics. ([mbie.govt.nz](https://www.mbie.govt.nz/about/news/concepts-selected-for-ai-research-platform?utm_source=openai))  
- **AI Forum NZ — AEC Working Group submission on Draft National Infrastructure Plan (dated 6 August 2025).**
  - Public submission to Te Waihanga emphasises digital enablement and AI in infrastructure planning and operations (eg, AI for predictive risk modelling, real-time monitoring, and AI capability uplift across the infrastructure sector). ([aiforum.org.nz](https://aiforum.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/AI-Forum-AEC-WG-Submission-on-National-Infrastructure-Plan-Aug-2025-.pdf))

**Case Studies**

1) **ML flood-inundation nowcasting for emergency response and infrastructure risk decisions (NIWA / Earth Sciences New Zealand; published 9 January 2025 and NIWA site content current in 2025)**  
- **Context:** Physical flood models can take up to ~24 hours to run, limiting usefulness in fast-moving events; need for rapid inundation mapping to support response and built-asset risk decisions. ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
- **AI method:** Pre-trained machine learning model producing hourly flood inundation maps in **~1–2 minutes** (as reported). ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Application:** Near-real-time hazard intelligence for flood preparedness/response in Westport framing, with intent to extend to other high-risk locations. ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Observed outcomes/impacts (publicly stated):** Callaghan Innovation story reports **95% accuracy** for inundation area forecasting in the Westport/Buller case framing and an average error under 0.1m per cell (as stated). ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  

2) **Predictive network operations for wastewater (Watercare; 18 August 2025 and 03 September 2025)**  
- **Context:** Reduce wastewater overflows and improve proactive maintenance via dense sensing and AI analytics across Auckland’s wastewater network. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
- **AI method:** Watercare describes an **AI analytics system** analysing high‑volume sensor data and other monitoring systems, plus contextual inputs (weather, tide, groundwater) to detect anomalies. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
- **Application:** Rollout of **5,000 sensors** to detect irregularities such as sudden level rises that may indicate blockages or inflow/infiltration. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
- **Observed outcomes/impacts (publicly stated):** Watercare reports a sensor detected a developing blockage in Ōtara and crews responded the same day, preventing an overflow. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-smart-sensors-prevent-wastewater-overflow-in-otara?utm_source=openai))  

3) **Computer-vision / ML safety analytics on civil works (Hutt City Council + Downer + RUSH Digital; published 8 May 2025)**  
- **Context:** Improve safety for workers and the public at the Tupua Horo Nuku seawall/shared path worksite. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
- **AI method:** Machine learning / computer vision event detection using on-site AI camera(s). ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
- **Application:** Detects falls (horizontal posture), intrusions into no‑go zones, PPE usage, worker vs public, and traffic speed in temporary traffic management. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
- **Observed outcomes/impacts (publicly stated):** Described as supplementing human decisions with real-time data; council states privacy protection is built in with reference to NZ privacy settings (Privacy Act and guidance from the Office of the Privacy Commissioner). ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  

4) **LLM-enabled council information access (“Ask Auckland Council”; last updated 27 February 2025)**  
- **Context:** High volume of resident enquiries and fragmented information across multiple council digital platforms. ([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))

([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Application:** Resident-facing information navigation, with multilingual ambitions aligned to New Zealand’s official languages (as stated in the article). ([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Observed outcomes/impacts (publicly stated):** Expected to improve customer experience and help people find information more easily; the article states the tool was expected to launch later in 2025. ([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))  

5) **Local-government AI governance and scaling artefacts (Hutt City Council “AI at Council”; page crawled December 2025)**

- **Observed outcomes/impacts (publicly stated):** Council frames outcomes as productivity and service-delivery improvements; public quantitative impacts are not provided. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/council/our-projects/ai-at-council?utm_source=openai))

**Trends and Outlook**

- **Operational AI is clearest in asset-owner operations and hazard intelligence, not yet broadly evidenced in core project delivery**
  - In this window, the most substantiated deployments relate to **water networks**, **hazard nowcasting**, and **council service/information interfaces** rather than AI-native design authoring, automated compliance checking, or end-to-end construction planning. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
- **“Instrument, then predict” is emerging as a scalable pattern for infrastructure operators**
  - Watercare’s programme reflects the operational logic of combining dense sensing with AI anomaly detection to shift from reactive response to earlier intervention; public evidence for similar scaling in other NZ asset classes (roads, buildings, energy) is limited in this period. ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
- **Computer vision is emerging for worksite safety, but public performance/impact metrics remain thin**
  - NZ evidence supports feasibility of event classes (falls, PPE, intrusion, speed) but does not provide detection accuracy in operational conditions or quantified reductions in incidents. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
- **Local government is becoming a visible ‘lead adopter’ of GenAI governance patterns**
  - Hutt City Council’s public AI register/strategy/policy and secure licence rollout is a notable NZ public transparency signal; this may influence expectations for councils and AEC-adjacent agencies that deploy GenAI affecting public outcomes. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/council/our-projects/ai-at-council?utm_source=openai))  
- **Public-sector governance is tightening and becoming more operationally specific**
  - The Public Service AI Framework and the GenAI guidance suite indicate an environment where transparency, accountability, security, and lifecycle management are becoming first-order constraints on scaling AI in AEC-adjacent public functions. ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/public-service-artificial-intelligence-framework?trk=public_post_comment-text&utm_source=openai))  
- **Startup signals for NZ AEC are still uneven in non-promotional evidence**
  - The strongest non-promotional evidence remains council-described deployments/pilots (eg, RUSH Digital in the Hutt City Council safety pilot). Other startup claims exist publicly but are often marketing-led and not consistently accompanied by independently verifiable outcomes in this window. ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
- **Early signals for business-model change in NZ AEC remain weakly evidenced**
  - Public, verifiable evidence of widespread consultancy fee-model changes, automated delivery at scale, or procurement transformation driven by AI is limited within the window; the more concrete value capture is in operational decision support for asset owners and public agencies. ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/dmsdocument/263~full-results-2025-cross-agency-survey-for-artificial-intelligence-ai-use-cases/html?utm_source=openai))

**A Global Perspective**

- **Major global trend: AI regulation and assurance is increasingly shaping how AEC-adjacent AI is deployed**
  - The **EU AI Act** entered into force **1 August 2024** and applies progressively, with key milestones including GPAI obligations applying from **2 August 2025** and broad applicability from **2 August 2026** (with specific exceptions). This is relevant globally for AEC firms and vendors operating across jurisdictions because it increases the compliance and assurance burden for certain AI uses (including customer-facing systems and higher-risk contexts). ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai?utm_source=openai))  
- **Global governance signal: management-system approaches for AI are becoming normalised**
  - **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** (AI management systems) provides a structured management-system standard for organisations that provide or use AI-based products/services, reflecting a global shift toward auditable AI governance practices that can be applied in safety- and risk-sensitive sectors such as AEC. ([iso.org](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html?utm_source=openai))  
- **Construction ‘Physical AI’ and autonomy is accelerating globally (heavy equipment and robotics)**
  - Global reporting highlights rapid development of AI-enabled autonomy in off‑highway equipment and construction machinery, with vendors and startups retrofitting or building systems using computer vision and autonomy stacks to address labour constraints and productivity (examples include autonomous/AI-enabled equipment announcements and startup funding narratives). ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/technology/deere-boosts-bet-autonomous-tractors-with-new-machines-2025-01-06/?utm_source=openai))  
- **AI for permitting and plan review is emerging internationally (government-side adoption)**
  - International municipal/county initiatives increasingly describe AI-assisted pre-checks and workflow acceleration in building permits, typically framed as assistive rather than replacing human reviewers—suggesting a pathway where AEC regulatory interfaces become AI-augmented (noting these are international signals, not NZ evidence). ([houstonchronicle.com](https://www.houstonchronicle.com/politics/houston/article/harris-county-ai-permitting-21171315.php?utm_source=openai))  
- **Global AEC application hotspots (consistent across jurisdictions)**
  - Worksite safety computer vision, autonomous equipment, and back-office document automation are recurring focal areas; public measurement of impact varies widely, and the strongest evidence often comes from controlled pilots or vendor-led reporting rather than independent evaluation. ([forbes.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/alanohnsman/2025/07/16/waymo-vets-are-automating-construction-sites-with-self-driving-dirt-diggers//?utm_source=openai))

**Conclusion**

- **Overall assessment (as at 18 December 2025).** Within the **18 December 2024–18 December 2025** evidence window, New Zealand’s AEC system shows credible, AI-explicit adoption concentrated in:
  - **Hazard intelligence:** ML flood inundation nowcasting with a stated path to operationalisation. ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Infrastructure operations:** AI-enabled anomaly detection and proactive response in wastewater networks (Watercare). ([watercare.co.nz](https://www.watercare.co.nz/home/about-us/latest-news-and-media/watercare-s-wastewater-network-smartens-up-with-rollout-of-5000-sensors?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Worksite safety (pilot):** Computer vision / ML event detection at a civil works site (Hutt City Council / Downer / RUSH Digital). ([huttcity.govt.nz](https://www.huttcity.govt.nz/people-and-communities/news/2025/ai-driving-safety-changes-at-tupua-horo-nuku?utm_source=openai))  
  - **Council information access and internal productivity:** LLM-enabled assistants and GenAI licences in local government. ([ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz](https://ourauckland.aucklandcouncil.govt.nz/news/2025/02/auckland-council-ai-initiative-to-boost-customer-experience/?utm_source=openai))  
  These deployments sit alongside a maturing governance context (public service AI framework and GenAI guidance) and a major national investment signal (NZIAT AI Research Platform concepts selection on 18 December 2025). ([digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/public-service-artificial-intelligence-framework?trk=public_post_comment-text&utm_source=openai))  
- **Strategic insights derived from evidence (non-prescriptive):**
  - Adoption is clearest where **risk exposure is high** and **timeliness + data volume** overwhelm traditional processes (hazards, network operations, safety monitoring). ([callaghaninnovation.govt.nz](https://www.callaghaninnovation.govt.nz/stories/ai-flood-forecasting-in-real-time/?utm_source=openai))  
  - For AEC-adjacent public functions, **trust, transparency, privacy and security** requirements are increasingly explicit and are shaping how AI is trialled and disclosed.
